Learn about the Geomagnetic Strom





This storm was the most powerful to affect Earth since March 1989, causing stunning aurorae displays at far more equatorial latitudes than usual in both the Northern and Southern Hemispheres.

Aurora Sightings Around the World:


- North America: Aurorae were spotted across the United States, as far south as the Florida Keys, Mexico's Yucatán Peninsula, The Bahamas, Jamaica, and Puerto Rico ¹.
- Europe: The aurora was visible from Portugal, Spain, Sardinia, Algeria, and the Canary Islands ¹.
- Asia: Aurorae were seen in Turkey, Cyprus, Iran, Japan, northern India, South Korea, and across northern China ¹.
- Australia and New Zealand: The aurora was visible as far north as Townsville and Mackay in Queensland, and across New Zealand ¹.
- South America and Africa: Aurorae were spotted in Chile, Argentina, South Africa, and as far north as New Caledonia, Uruguay, southern Brazil, and Namibia ¹.

The storm's impact was significant, causing disruptions to radio communications, power grids, and satellite services. However, widespread power outages were avoided thanks to preparations by power companies ¹.
